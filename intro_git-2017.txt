Instructor: Tim Rice (timothy.rice@unimelb.edu.au, @resnomicon on Twitter, cryptarch on Github)
Helper: Errol Lloyd (@maegul everywhere)

Workshop structure:

1. Background of Git, motivation for using it.
2. Introduction to GitHub: create acct, create repo, markdown, README, licences and forking, viewing history, branching and merging, "pull requests".
3. Introduction to Unix Command Line.
4. A peek under Git's hood: hashes, objects, index, HEAD.
5. Simple local Git: init, status, add, commit, diff, reset, log.
6. Working with branches: branch, checkout, merge.
7. Working with history.
7. Working with remote repos: clone, remote, fetch, pull, push.
8. Common workflows: fork & pull, branch & merge.
9. Advanced configuration: better logging, aliases, ignoring things.
